Welcome!

It's nice to meet you.


I'm Sara Coren. I am a product driven full stack web developer excited about expanding my knowledge base in the field. I have been a life-long learner and elementary educator as well as a researcher, geoscientist and humanitarian. I am passionate about using my web development skills and human centered approach in design to help people around the world access information to improve the quality of their lives.

As a life-long lover of the sciences, I recevied my undergraduate degree in Geology followed by a Masters degree in Geography. I combined my passions for the environmental sciences, mapping and local community building by studying natural hazards and disaster reduction around the world. This led to working as an information management unit intern at the United Nations in International Strategy for Disaster Reduction (UN-ISDR) in Geneva, Switzerland.

My love of sharing information with others inspired me to get a second Masters degree in Elementary Education and I spent eight years teaching in a variety of classroom settings from grades pre-K to 8 in public schools in New York City and private and STEM schools in Connecticut. I have a plethora of experience developing my own curriculum to meet the needs of a wide reange of student learners and I am particularly interested in promoting STEM education for the next generation. My classroom experience also drew out my inate abilites to manage large groups of individuals and train teams of student and assistant teachers to implement best practices.

My most recent pursuits involve doing a Software Engineering Immersive course with General Assembly. I am really enjoying combining my propensity for UX Design with my problem-sovling drive to code creative solutions to technical challenges. I have experience building sites as a full stack developer, but I do feel particulary drawn to frontend work.
